Waypoint Institute

Waypoint is a donor-funded, tuition-free Christian education project centered on a great-books core in Scripture, classical theology, and the liberal arts. Cohort-based online delivery, a curated public-domain library, and transfer-friendly partnerships keep the focus on formation and rigorous study. Certificates exist, but the program’s emphasis is education rather than credentialism.

Formation of Supernatural Agents in Dreams Through Simulation: A Grounded Cognition Perspective

Doctoral dissertation (ABD, National University; proposal stage 2025). Tests whether dream “simulation-richness” predicts religiosity (BMMRS) and paranormal belief (RPBS) using ~1,200 dream reports over 10–14 days (n=120; DREEM subsample n=60 with REM metrics). Codes dreams for sensorimotor simulation, agentic figures, and narrative structure; evaluates REM moderation. Chair: Patrick McNamara.

The Legend of Zelda and Religion (edited volume)

A multi-author volume arguing that religious forms in Zelda emerge from within the game world—its mechanics, spaces, symbols, and narratives. Topics include ritual performance, sacred time/space, normativity, technology and landscape, player phenomenology, and theological motifs. Prospectus lists 18 contributors; 72–90k words; submission to series editor by March 1, 2026; target publication near the March 2027 film release.
