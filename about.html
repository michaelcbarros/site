Research Overview

Michael C. Barros is an interdisciplinary scholar whose work explores how myth, ritual, and sacred imagination emerge within contemporary media. He is currently ABD in Psychology at National University, where his dissertation examines the cognitive formation of supernatural agents in dreams through grounded cognition theory.

He holds a Master’s degree in Biblical and Theological Studies and has taught humanities, philosophy, and psychology at both secondary and postsecondary levels. His research integrates theology, cognitive science, and cultural analysis, with publications on religion in video games, Philip K. Dick, archetypal criticism, and media theory.

He serves as co-editor of The Esoteric Theology of Philip K. Dick (Bloomsbury, forthcoming 2025) and has published in venues including Academia Letters, The Classical Connection, and SSHJ.
